Best Practices in Web Scraping

These guidelines help ensure your scraping is efficient, ethical, and reliable.
  • Respect robots.txt and site terms: Always check if the site allows scraping before sending requests.
  • Rate limiting: Throttle requests to avoid overloading servers; use polite delays and retries.
  • User-Agent headers: Identify your scraper responsibly instead of pretending to be a browser.
  • Handle errors gracefully: Implement retries with backoff, and log errors for debugging.
  • Data cleanliness: Validate, deduplicate, and sanitize scraped data before storage.
  • Scalability: Use queues (Celery, Redis, RabbitMQ) for managing large scraping jobs.
  • Security: Avoid executing raw HTML/JS; sanitize inputs to protect your pipeline.
  • Monitoring: Track scraper health, request counts, and failures to detect issues early.
  • Storage strategy: Choose proper formats (CSV, JSON, database) depending on use case.
  • Legal considerations: Ensure compliance with copyright, privacy, and data regulations.
